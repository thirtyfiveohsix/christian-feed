SaaS businesses that benefit from MDM-style thinking

  • Sales-led B2B SaaS pipeline → revenue lag
    • When the gap between forecast and target is really an unspoken bet on pipeline coverage, win rates, cycle time, and onboarding time-to-value.
  • PLG / self-serve SaaS activation mechanics
    • Where “conversion” is actually a chain: visit → signup → activation → habit → upgrade, with time lags and cohort decay.
  • Usage-based / infra SaaS value realization
    • Revenue is downstream of adoption intensity; cohorts + expansion/contraction dominate naive MRR models.
  • Vertical SaaS retention + workflows
    • Churn is often workflow displacement (not “CS problems”); decomposition forces you to name the mechanism.
  • Fintech / payments SaaS unit economics
    • Gross margin, take-rate, risk, and funding constraints make “growth” a system, not a line chart.
  • Marketplace-ish SaaS two-sided volume
    • Supply/demand matching makes the Volume/Value/Time-to-Value triangle explicit (and easy to lie about without a model).
Heuristic: if outcomes are lagged, blended, or cohort-dependent, MDM beats dashboard theater.
